What Happened

The clouds moved on.

There were a few hard days between us — the kind that feel bigger at night than they are in the morning. But nothing is ever too heavy for God to carry, and He didn't leave the two of us to carry it alone either.

Jayshree stood in the middle of it with patience, and Ammu's brother stepped in the way an elder brother should — not to take sides, but to help two people who care about each other find their way back to being kind. I'm grateful for both of them, quietly and genuinely.

And then Ammu did what Ammu does — she chose to stay soft instead of staying hurt. She let me feel, again, that she isn't going anywhere. That safety is a gift I don't take lightly.

"A friend loveth at all times, and a brother is born for adversity." — Proverbs 17:17
My Resolve — Not a Promise

I won't promise you. I'll show you.

The Bible is clear that we're not to swear oaths over ourselves — our own word isn't strong enough to stand on, and tomorrow isn't ours to guarantee. So I won't tell you "I promise." I'll tell you something smaller and more honest instead: today, by God's grace, I will try to be better than I was. And tomorrow, God willing, I'll try again.

That's not a loophole to avoid responsibility — it's the opposite. It means I'm not hiding behind a big word I can't fully control. I'm choosing, daily, to grow, to listen better, to guard my temper, and to never let the same mistake repeat itself on your heart again. Not because I swore it, but because I mean it, one day at a time, with God as the One actually holding me to it.

"Let your communication be, Yea, yea; Nay, nay: for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil." — Matthew 5:37 · "For that ye ought to say, If the Lord will, we shall live, and do this, or that." — James 4:15

A prayer for you, Ammu

God, thank You for her softness even after it was tested. Keep her heart light. Give her the kind of peace that doesn't depend on how the day went. Remind her, on the days she doubts it, that she is deeply known and deeply chosen — by You first, and by me too.

A prayer for me

God, don't let me get comfortable and slow to notice her again. Give me patience before I have to look for it. Make me quicker to say sorry and slower to need to be right. Keep shaping me into someone worth her trust, one ordinary day at a time.

Where We're Actually Headed

Not just through life. Into heaven, together.

Every fight we survive is small next to the one thing I actually want for us — that we'd stay close to God on the way, and end up in the same place He's preparing. That's the real promise being kept here. Not mine. His.
